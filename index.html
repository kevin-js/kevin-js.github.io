About Me

As you've probably figured from its omnipresence on the page, my name is Kevin Junkai Guh. I was born and raised in sunny Santa Monica, California (with brief stints living/working in Taiwan and Italy) and somehow managed to end up at the polar opposite of Hanover, New Hampshire, where I am now a senior at Dartmouth College studying computer science, mathematics, and economics. I am currently searching for post-graduation positions as a data scientist, product manager, and/or software engineer/developer so if you have an opportunity, leave me a message below and I'd be happy to start a conversation!

Kevin the Engineer


Growing up as the son of an architect and a structural engineering professor, I was very much immersed in a building environment (literally and figuratively) and still bear much of that culture with me to this day. There is some fundamental beauty in making something out of nothing, be it a skyscraper or website, and in the process of discovery and creation. As a founder and director of HackDartmouth, Dartmouth College's student-run hackathon program, I seek to share this passion for building with my peers by bringing students, mentors, and sponsors from around the world to work on innovative projects together. And now, for a shameless plug: if you or anyone you know would be interested in being a part of this program, please check out our site here and feel free to reach out to myself or our general team account.

As much as for the creative aspect of engineering, I also enjoy building things because of the social component in teammwork. Oddly enough, some of my most vivid memories in college are of energy-drink and adrenaline fueled 4AM coding sessions with my teammates debating just how "lenient" we can be with our program features to get it turned in by the 10AM deadline. Ultimately, I see engineering as building with people for people and that is why I love it.


Kevin the Musician


Yup, I was one of those kids who was signed up by my parents for piano lessons as soon as I learned walk. And to be honest, I absolutely detested it, kicking and screaming my way to every lesson for those first few years. I have to credit the SMMUSD music program for ultimately showing me that there's more to music than hacking one's way through the Grade 3 repetoire and opening my eyes to what has become a life-long hobby. I played the clarinet and saxophone in middle/high school band, and after getting sidetracked cleaning out the garage one afternoon ended up learning to play guitar on my dad's creaky old Esteve. Heck, I even ended up in a heavy metal band for a bit (guess all that kicking and screaming as a kid came in handy) and formed another alternative-Latin-funk-rock band with some friends in high school called ¿Dónde Está Mi Camiseta?

I still try to write and play music whenever I can and the more I hear, the more I experiment. Currently, I am fascinated by the concept of algorithmic music, which is effectively the application of machine learning algorithms and models to create music. I've also taken to posting some snippets of my compositions on SoundCloud, so check them out below if you're interested!
